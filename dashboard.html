Cadence

Steps run in order. The Wait is measured from when the previous step completed and can be set in seconds, minutes, hours, or days. A message step automatically waits until the invite is accepted before it fires. Merge tags: {first} {last} {name} {company} {title} (+ {First} {Company} …). Note: the between-action safety pace (Min/Max gap below) still applies, so very short waits won't fire faster than that.

Safety limits

These caps are your account's seatbelt. LinkedIn's hard ceilings in 2026 are roughly ~100 connection invites/week and ~150 total actions/day; the defaults sit well under them. Warm-up ramps you up gradually. Lower is safer.

About & safety

OutreachOS runs entirely in your own browser on the real LinkedIn page — no cloud, no session-cookie export to a datacenter (the mismatch that most often flags accounts). It calls LinkedIn's own internal APIs the same way the site does, on a randomized human-paced schedule, with per-action daily/weekly caps, a warm-up ramp, working-hours windows, and an instant halt on any captcha or limit wall.

This does not make automation "allowed." Automating LinkedIn violates its User Agreement (§8.2) and can get your account restricted or banned. Use conservatively, for authorized outreach only, on an account you accept the risk of losing. Start with Dry run and a tiny daily cap.
